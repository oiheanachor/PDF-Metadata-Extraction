Why the Revision Comparison Is Critical to Successful Bluestar Deployment
The value of the revision extraction process is that it creates a three-way validation loop:

PDF revision evidence — what is actually visible on the Engineering drawing.
Legacy system revision evidence — what the historical source system reports, such as MTMS or other legacy extracts.
Current folder / file-state evidence — what the active Engineering folder structure suggests is currently in use.

When these sources agree, Bluestar can be updated with higher confidence. When they disagree, the migration team has a clear exception list for investigation before data is loaded, corrected, released, or used in testing. This aligns with migration precondition guidance, which stresses the need to understand what data exists, what must be migrated, whether requirements differ by site, whether CAD systems differ, whether files overlap, and whether filename/file-ID overlaps could create duplicate or incorrect data in Bluestar. [BS-Migrati...725-111219 | PDF], [BS-Migrati...725-111219 | PDF]
This comparison is also important because Bluestar deployment is not isolated to Engineering. Bluestar release settings and migration documentation connect engineering objects and BOMs to downstream release and D365/FinOps processes. The procedure for importing MTMS Super BOM data into Bluestar includes setup and release-related requirements, including company/site defaults and release behaviour, while migration process material references releasing objects and BOMs to FinOps from Bluestar. If revision values are wrong at the engineering-object level, the impact can propagate into BOM accuracy, product availability, release decisions, and downstream operational use.
